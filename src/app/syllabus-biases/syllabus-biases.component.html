Cognitive Biases

A cognitive bias is a systematic pattern of deviation from norm or rationality in judgment. Individuals create their own "subjective reality" from their perception of the input. An individual's construction of reality, not the objective input, may dictate their behavior in the world.

Confirmation Bias

The tendency to seek out evidence consistent with one's views, and to ignore, dismiss, or selectively reinterpret evidence that contradicts them

Naive Realism

The tendency to believe the world is exactly as we see it.

Bias Blind Spots

The belief that only Others are biased and not oneself.

False Concensus Effect

The tendency to overestimate the extent to which Others share our Views.

Insider Perspective

Prevents individuals from understanding or even considering how Outsiders might assess a certain situation.
